# MiLB Rookie Leagues: ACL, FCL, and DSL

**Sources:** [mlb.com/milb/arizona-complex](https://www.mlb.com/milb/arizona-complex), [mlb.com/milb/florida-complex](https://www.mlb.com/milb/florida-complex), [mlb.com/milb/dominican-summer](https://www.mlb.com/milb/dominican-summer)

## Summary

The three Rookie-level circuits in Minor League Baseball — the Arizona Complex League (ACL), Florida Complex League (FCL), and Dominican Summer League (DSL) — serve as the entry-level development tier for MLB organizations. Games are typically played at Spring Training complexes (U.S.) or academy facilities (Dominican Republic), often with free admission. These leagues feature the youngest and newest professional prospects.

## Dominican Summer League (DSL)

- **Operating since:** 1985
- **Location:** Dominican Republic (academy complexes)
- **2026 Opening Day:** June 1
- **Operated by:** Major League Baseball
- **Headquarters:** 1271 Avenue of the Americas, New York, NY 10020

### DSL Teams

The DSL has the largest footprint of any Rookie league, with most MLB organizations fielding one or two squads. Teams include DSL affiliates for: Arizona (Black/Red), Athletics, Braves, Orioles (Black/Orange), Red Sox (Blue/Red), Cubs (Blue/Red), and many more — roughly 40+ total squads.

### 2025 DSL Cup

The DSL introduced a new postseason format in 2025 — the **DSL Cup** — replacing the prior championship format. Sixteen of the top DSL teams participated in a two-stage playoff (pool play → best-of-3 semifinals/finals). DSL Padres Gold won the inaugural DSL Cup, defeating DSL Athletics 12–5 in the decisive Game 2.

### Notable DSL Prospects (2026)

- **Josuar Gonzalez** — DSL Giants Black (2025 international free agent)
- **Kevin Alvarez** — DSL Astros Blue (2025 international free agent)
- **Elian Peña** — DSL Mets Orange (2025 international free agent; $5M signing bonus, second only to Roki Sasaki for the 2025 international period; hit three homers in a game twice)

## Key Observations Across Rookie Leagues

- All three leagues serve as **entry-level development** — tools-first, makeup-focused evaluation (source: [minor-league-baseball-ecosystem.md](minor-league-baseball-ecosystem.md))
- The ACL and FCL were rebranded in 2021 from AZL and GCL, respectively, as part of MLB's post-2020 minor league restructuring
- Both U.S. complex leagues share a manager (Andy Shultz) and open on the same date (May 2, 2026); the DSL opens later (June 1, 2026)
- Several organizations field **multiple squads** in each league (identified by color designations: Black/Red, Blue/Orange, East/West, etc.)
- Games are played at Spring Training complexes with free admission (U.S.) or at academy facilities (DR)
- The DSL's new Cup format (2025) added a structured 16-team postseason to the league for the first time
