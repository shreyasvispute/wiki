# Minor League Baseball Ecosystem: League Levels, Development Pathways, and Scouting Workflows

**Source:** PDF document (raw/assets/)

## Summary

A comprehensive reference document covering the full Minor League Baseball ecosystem — league levels and their characteristics, player entry and progression pathways, scouting roles and evaluation methods, decision frameworks for promotions/demotions, and practical tools for scouting intelligence.

---

## League Levels Overview

| Level | Competition Quality | Typical Age | Public Statcast | Dominant Scouting Focus |
|-------|-------------------|-------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Rookie / Complex (DSL, ACL, FCL)** | Entry-level development | Teens–early 20s | Not broadly available | Tools, makeup, athletic traits |
| **Low-A (Single-A)** | Full-season intro | Late teens–early 20s | Limited (subset of parks) | Approach, strike-zone control, age-relative performance |
| **High-A** | Full-season advanced | Early–mid 20s | Limited | Skill consolidation, role hypotheses |
| **Double-A** | Upper minors — pivotal level | Mid 20s | None in public dataset (cited snapshot) | Breaking ball recognition, command, 40-man/Rule 5 timing |
| **Triple-A** | MLB-adjacent depth | Mid–late 20s | Complete public tracking | MLB role readiness, call-up translation |
| **Independent leagues** | Varies; "second chance" pro | Wide range (often older) | Varies | Pro readiness, showcase, re-entry |
| **International pro leagues (NPB, KBO, etc.)** | High-level pro | Wide range | Varies by league | Pro performance, role definition |

## Player Entry & Progression Pathways

### Entry Points into Affiliated Baseball

- All minor leaguers are employees under an MLB organization via contract and assignment
- MiLB teams do **not** independently try out players — players are under MLB organizational control
- Two major entry funnels:
  1. **Drafted players (Rule 4 / "MLB Draft")** — signed after draft, assigned to an affiliate; newly drafted players often begin in controlled developmental environments
  2. **International amateur signings** — eligible at age 16, sign during annual windows; frequently begin in Dominican Republic academy ecosystem and/or DSL pathway

### Common Progression Tracks

- **International teen signings:** DSL → Complex League (ACL/FCL) → Low-A → High-A → Double-A → Triple-A → MLB
- **U.S. high school draftees:** Complex/Rookie → Low-A → High-A → Double-A → Triple-A → MLB
- **College draftees:** Low-A or High-A (sometimes skipping complex) → Double-A → Triple-A → MLB (often faster)

### Typical Timelines

- **"Fast track" college draftees** (especially polished pitchers): ~1–3 years to MLB
- **High school draftees and teen international signings:** ~3–7+ years to MLB

### Alternate Routes

- **Independent leagues** as re-entry channel — players can have contracts purchased by MLB clubs
- **International pro leagues** as pathways — Japanese Posting System (NPB → MLB), Korean Posting System (KBO → MLB)

## Scouting Roles

| Role | Focus | Level Emphasis |
|------|-------|---------------|
| **Area scouts** | Amateur acquisition | Pre-draft, spillover into early pro evaluation |
| **Cross-checkers** | Calibration across territories; sit between area scouts and scouting director | Amateur → early pro; key for high-uncertainty young players |
| **Pro scouts / advance scouts** | Professional acquisition and valuation; includes international pro evaluation | Upper minors, trade targets, external acquisitions |
| **Player development staff** | Internal evaluation for promotion/role decisions; year-round system | All levels — highest intensity at Rookie/Complex/Low-A |

### Role Interaction by Level

- **Rookie/Complex/Low-A:** More player-development staff intensity; scouting focus on tools and makeup; fewer outside pro-scout looks except for top prospects
- **High-A/Double-A:** Heavier cross-checking and pro-scout attention; increased evaluation for 40-man/Rule 5 timing
- **Triple-A:** Frequent pro-scout looks (trade/waiver depth), plus MLB staff monitoring for call-up readiness

## Evaluation Methods

### 20–80 Scouting Scale

- Industry standard; 50 = average, 60 = above average, 70–80 = well above average
- Classic five tools for position players: **hit, power, run, field, throw**
- "Future overall" grade is key for prospect projection
- Pitcher evaluation: fastball, best secondary, other pitches, command, control

### Key Tracked Measures

- **Spin Rate:** RPM at release; affects pitch trajectory and movement perception
- **Launch Angle:** Vertical angle off the bat (degrees)
- **Exit Velocity:** Hard-hit ball defined as EV ≥ 95 mph
- **Sprint Speed:** Statcast framework with elite thresholds
- **Plate Discipline:** O-Swing%, Z-Swing%, Z-Contact%, K%, BB%

### Public Tracking Data Gaps

- MLB's public Minor League Statcast dataset is **not complete**:
  - Complete Triple-A tracking (2023; partial 2022)
  - Limited Single-A tracking (subset of parks, back to 2021)
  - **No Double-A** in the described public dataset
  - Some defensive tracking (e.g., Outs Above Average) not tracked via Baseball Savant for minors

## Decision Framework: Promote / Hold / Demote / Release

Four key inputs for movement decisions:

1. **Player readiness signal** — performance + process + scouting grades; age/level context is critical; some stats are less predictive at low levels
2. **Health and workload** — pitcher innings, recovery, velocity maintenance; hitter availability
3. **Roster constraints and eligibility clocks:**
   - Domestic Reserve List tightened to **165** (in-season)
   - **40-man roster** required for MLB active roster eligibility
   - **Rule 5 Draft protection:** signed at 18 or younger → protect within 5 seasons; signed at 19+ → within 4 seasons
   - **Minor league options** affect movement flexibility for 40-man players
   - **Service time:** 172 days = 1 MLB service year; impacts free agency/arbitration timing
4. **Organizational context** — injuries, role needs, contending vs. rebuilding stance

### Operational Triggers

- **Promote:** Sustained performance + stable process + age-appropriate dominance; demonstrated readiness for next-level constraints; approaching Rule 5 exposure or 40-man decision
- **Hold:** Targeted development plan in progress (pitch design, swing decision goals)
- **Demote:** Overmatched (chase spikes, contact collapse, zone/velo loss) with a better-suited environment available
- **Release:** Underperformance + roster-cap pressure + low projection odds; limited contract slots prioritized for higher-upside players

## Practical Tools

### Scouting Report Template

1. **Player Identification** — Name, B-T, Pos, Age, Level, Org, Date, Opponent; physical description; background
2. **Tools/Stuff (20–80)** — Present and future grades for each tool or pitch
3. **Data Snapshot** — 14d/30d line, plate discipline, batted ball, pitch shape/velo, defense/baserunning
4. **Mechanics & Athletic Movement** — Load, bat path, delivery, deception, repeatability
5. **Approach & Makeup** — Game plan adherence, adjustment history, competitive traits
6. **Risk & Development Plan** — Primary risk factors, next development focus (1–3 items)
7. **Projection & ETA** — Most likely MLB role, realistic outcome band, timeline, key decision checkpoints

### Weekly/Monthly Checklists by Level

| Level | Weekly Focus | Monthly Focus |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| **Rookie** | 20–80 present/future tool updates w/ evidence; routine readiness indicators; directional BB%/K% | Reconcile grades with measurements; promotion readiness memo |
| **Low-A** | Plate discipline snapshot (O-Swing%, Z-Contact%, K%, BB%); age-relative context; pitch-shape review | Contact quality evaluation (EV/LA); promotion case write-up |
| **High-A** | Pitch mix stability & plan execution; approach persistence vs better pitching | Role hypothesis (starter/reliever, everyday/platoon); Double-A readiness |
| **Double-A** | Breaking/offspeed chase control; command consistency; "roster clock" check (Rule 5, 40-man) | 40-man protection memo |
| **Triple-A** | MLB-role readiness checklist (starter: 2–3 pitches + command; reliever: elite bat-misser + strikes; hitter: offensive role + defensive fit); option/service-time flags | "Call-up packet" per priority player |

## Recommendations for Scouting Intelligence Platform

1. **Level-aware evaluation schema:** Store 20–80 grades with evidence strings, stats with age/level baselines, and process metrics where public
2. **Decision gate modules:** Rule 5/40-man timing, options/service time tracking, roster-cap pressure (165 Domestic Reserve List)
3. **Standardized coverage plans:** Minimum look counts by player tier and level; explicit uncertainty recording; layered evaluation consistent with cross-checking concepts
