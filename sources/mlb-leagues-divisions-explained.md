# Major League Baseball Leagues & Divisions, Explained

**Author:** Matthew Jackson | **Published:** Mar 3, 2026 | **Source:** NBC Insider

## Summary

An overview of MLB's organizational structure aimed at new fans ahead of Sunday Night Baseball's debut on NBC and Peacock.

## Key Points

- MLB has **30 teams** divided into two leagues of 15 each: the **American League (AL)** and the **National League (NL)**
- Each league is subdivided into three divisions of five teams: **East**, **Central**, and **West**
- Teams play most of their games against division rivals, with interleague play mixed in for balance
- The regular season consists of **162 games** per team, with a mid-summer break for the All-Star Game

## Team Alignment

### American League

| Division | Teams |
|----------|-------|
| AL East | Baltimore Orioles, Boston Red Sox, New York Yankees, Tampa Bay Rays, Toronto Blue Jays |
| AL Central | Chicago White Sox, Cleveland Guardians, Detroit Tigers, Kansas City Royals, Minnesota Twins |
| AL West | Athletics (moving to Las Vegas by 2028), Houston Astros, Los Angeles Angels, Seattle Mariners, Texas Rangers |

### National League

| Division | Teams |
|----------|-------|
| NL East | Atlanta Braves, Miami Marlins, New York Mets, Philadelphia Phillies, Washington Nationals |
| NL Central | Chicago Cubs, Cincinnati Reds, Milwaukee Brewers, Pittsburgh Pirates, St. Louis Cardinals |
| NL West | Arizona Diamondbacks, Colorado Rockies, Los Angeles Dodgers, San Diego Padres, San Francisco Giants |

## Postseason Format

- **12 teams** make the playoffs (6 per league)
- The three **division champions** from each league earn automatic entry
- The next three best records per league earn **wild card** spots
- The division champion with the worst record plays in the Wild Card round
- The other two division champions per league receive a **first-round bye**

## Notable Details

- The Athletics are noted as formerly of Oakland, moving to Las Vegas by 2028
- The structure mirrors the NFL's two-conference format (source: [mlb-leagues-divisions-explained.md](mlb-leagues-divisions-explained.md))
