## Rookie Leagues

Three Rookie-level leagues are shared across multiple organizations (not one-to-one affiliated). Games are played at Spring Training complexes (U.S.) or academy facilities (DR) with free admission.

### Dominican Summer League (DSL)

- **Operating since:** 1985
- **Location:** Dominican Republic (academy complexes)
- **2026 Opening Day:** June 1
- **Operated by:** Major League Baseball

The DSL has the largest footprint of any Rookie league, with most MLB organizations fielding 1–2 squads (~40+ total). In 2025, the DSL introduced the **DSL Cup** — a new 16-team playoff format (pool play → best-of-3 finals). DSL Padres Gold won the inaugural DSL Cup, defeating DSL Athletics 12–5.

**Recent Champions:** 2025 DSL Padres Gold (first DSL Cup) · 2024 DSL Red Sox Red · 2023 DSL Dodgers Bautista · 2022 DSL Phillies White · 2021 DSL Blue Jays *(no 2020 season — COVID-19)*

## Key Observations

- Every MLB org fields exactly **4 full-season affiliates** (120 total) plus squads in shared Rookie leagues
- Each level has exactly **30 teams**, matching the 30 MLB organizations one-to-one
- The 11 full-season leagues range from 6 teams (Northwest League) to 20 teams (International League)
- Several orgs field **multiple Rookie squads** per league (color-coded: Black/Red, Blue/Orange, East/West)
- Geographic clustering is common for player-movement efficiency (e.g., Cardinals: Memphis → Springfield → Peoria → Palm Beach)
- Some affiliates share parent club names (Iowa Cubs, Syracuse Mets), while most have unique identities
- ACL and FCL were rebranded in 2021 (from AZL/GCL) as part of MLB's post-2020 restructuring
