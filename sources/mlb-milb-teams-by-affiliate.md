# MLB Minor League Teams by Affiliate

**Source:** [mlb.com/milb/about/teams/by-affiliate](https://www.mlb.com/milb/about/teams/by-affiliate)

## Summary

A complete listing of all 120 full-season affiliated minor league teams organized by MLB parent club, spanning four levels (Triple-A, Double-A, High-A, Single-A), plus three Rookie-level leagues. Every MLB organization maintains exactly four full-season affiliates. (source: mlb.com)

## Rookie Leagues

Three Rookie-level leagues are shared across multiple organizations (not individually affiliated):

- **Arizona Complex League (ACL)**
- **Florida Complex League (FCL)**
- **Dominican Summer League (DSL)**

---

## Key Observations

- Every MLB organization fields exactly **4 full-season affiliates** (Triple-A through Single-A), for a total of **120 full-season teams**
- Some affiliate names mirror the parent club (Iowa Cubs, Dunedin Blue Jays, Syracuse Mets, etc.), while most have unique identities
- Rookie leagues operate under shared organizational structures rather than one-to-one affiliation
- Geographic clustering is common — several organizations place affiliates in regional corridors for player-movement efficiency (e.g., Cardinals' Memphis → Springfield → Peoria → Palm Beach)
