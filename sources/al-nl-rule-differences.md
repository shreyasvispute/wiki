# Rule Differences Between MLB's American League & National League

**Author:** Matthew Jackson | **Published:** Mar 18, 2026 | **Source:** NBC Insider

## Summary

An explainer on the historical and current rule differences between the AL and NL, concluding that as of 2026 there are no major rule differences remaining.

## Key Points

### History of the Two Leagues

- The **National League** is the older of the two leagues
- Both leagues grew out of the late-19th-century proliferation of baseball leagues across the U.S.
- By the end of the 19th century, the AL and NL were the clear dominant forces
- The leagues agreed to share the spotlight — joining for the **World Series** and sharing revenue
- For much of their history, the AL and NL operated as **largely autonomous units**
- Interleague play was not common outside the World Series
- Each league had its own character:
  - **AL** was known for scoring
  - **NL** was more of a pitcher's playground, focused on strategy and defense

### The Merger

- At the end of the 20th century, the AL and NL **legally merged** into the modern MLB organization
- Impacts of the merger:
  - Interleague play became standard during the regular season
  - Players moved more freely between leagues via trades and free agency
  - Rules were **standardized** across both leagues

### Current Rule Differences (2026)

- **As of 2026, there are no major rule differences** between the AL and NL
- The last major difference was the **Designated Hitter (DH)** rule:
  - The AL adopted the DH in the **1970s**
  - The NL resisted the change for decades
  - In **2022**, universal DH rules were standardized across both leagues
