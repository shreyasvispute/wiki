# MLB Player Development Pathways

Player development in professional baseball follows structured progression tracks from entry into the system through to MLB debut. (source: [minor-league-baseball-ecosystem.md](../sources/minor-league-baseball-ecosystem.md))

## Entry Points

### Rule 4 Draft (MLB Draft)
- U.S. amateur players (high school and college) enter via the annual MLB Draft
- After signing, draftees are assigned to an affiliate at an appropriate developmental level
- Newly drafted players often begin in controlled environments (complex leagues)

### International Amateur Signings
- Players become eligible at **age 16** and sign during annual international signing windows
- Many begin in the **Dominican Republic academy ecosystem** and/or the **Dominican Summer League (DSL)**

### Other Routes
- **Independent leagues** serve as re-entry channels — players can be noticed and have contracts purchased by MLB clubs
- **International pro leagues** provide structured transfer paths: Japanese Posting System (NPB → MLB), Korean Posting System (KBO → MLB)

## Common Progression Tracks

| Background | Typical Path |
|-----------|-------------|
| International teen signing | DSL → Complex (ACL/FCL) → Low-A → High-A → Double-A → Triple-A → MLB |
| U.S. high school draftee | Complex/Rookie → Low-A → High-A → Double-A → Triple-A → MLB |
| College draftee | Low-A or High-A (may skip complex) → Double-A → Triple-A → MLB |

## Typical Timelines

- **"Fast track" college draftees** (especially polished pitchers): **~1–3 years** to MLB
- **High school draftees and teen international signings:** **~3–7+ years** to MLB, reflecting earlier entry age and longer physical/skill development windows

## Movement Model

Promotion/demotion decisions are modeled as a constrained optimization across four inputs:

1. **Player readiness** — performance + process data + scouting grades
2. **Health and workload** — innings, recovery, velocity maintenance
3. **Roster constraints** — 40-man roster, Rule 5 protection clocks, Domestic Reserve List (165), minor league options, service time (172 days = 1 year)
4. **Organizational context** — MLB injuries, contending vs. rebuilding priorities
