# MLB Roster Mechanics

The rules governing MLB and MiLB rosters create the constraints within which all player development and acquisition decisions operate. (source: [minor-league-baseball-ecosystem.md](../sources/minor-league-baseball-ecosystem.md))

## Key Roster Rules

### 40-Man Roster
- A player must be on the 40-man roster to be added to the MLB active roster
- Clubs manage space via DFA, trades, releases, and 60-day IL moves

### Domestic Reserve List
- Capped at **165** in-season
- Creates squeeze decisions on organizational-depth players and compresses evaluation windows

### Rule 5 Draft Protection
- Players signed at **age 18 or younger** must be added to the 40-man roster within **5 seasons** to be protected
- Players signed at **age 19+** must be protected within **4 seasons**
- Approaching Rule 5 exposure is a key trigger for accelerated promotions to evaluate players against tougher competition

### Minor League Options
- Affect the ease of moving 40-man roster players between MLB and the minors
- Players with remaining options can be sent down without exposure to waivers

### Service Time
- **172 days** on the MLB active roster = one MLB service year
- Impacts free agency and arbitration eligibility timing
- Can influence MLB call-up timing and roster planning

## Decision Triggers

These roster mechanics create specific decision gates in player development:

| Mechanism | Decision Impact |
|-----------|----------------|
| Rule 5 exposure approaching | Promote to evaluate; add to 40-man or risk losing player |
| 40-man roster full | DFA/trade/release to create space for incoming prospect |
| Domestic Reserve List at 165 | Must prioritize limited slots for highest-upside players; releases of depth players |
| Options remaining | Freely shuttle player between MLB and minors for development or depth |
| Service time accumulation | Timing of call-ups may be strategized around free agency clocks |
