# Minor League Baseball (MiLB)

Minor League Baseball is the system of professional baseball leagues and teams affiliated with [Major League Baseball](major-league-baseball.md). Every minor leaguer is an employee under an MLB organization via contract and assignment; MiLB teams do not independently try out players. (source: [minor-league-baseball-ecosystem.md](../sources/minor-league-baseball-ecosystem.md))

## League Levels

MiLB is organized into a hierarchy of levels, from developmental rookie ball to MLB-adjacent Triple-A:

| Level | Key Characteristics |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Rookie / Complex (DSL, ACL, FCL)** | Entry-level development; teens and young players; tools-first evaluation |
| **Low-A (Single-A)** | First full-season level; emphasis on approach and strike-zone control |
| **High-A** | Skill consolidation against better competition over a full season |
| **Double-A** | Beginning of the "upper minors"; pivotal talent and mental-preparation bar; heavy pro-scout attention |
| **Triple-A** | MLB-adjacent; call-up readiness; complete public Statcast tracking available |

- The **Dominican Summer League (DSL)** serves as the primary entry point for international teen signings; operating since 1985 in the Dominican Republic; opens June 1; introduced the DSL Cup playoff format in 2025
- **Arizona Complex League (ACL)** and **Florida Complex League (FCL)** serve U.S.-based complex/rookie development at Spring Training facilities with free admission; both open May 2; rebranded from AZL and GCL in 2021 as part of post-2020 restructuring
- Several organizations field **multiple Rookie squads** per league (identified by color: Black/Red, Blue/Orange, etc.)

For detailed team rosters, stadiums, champions, and notable prospects, see the [MiLB Rookie Leagues](../sources/milb-rookie-leagues.md) source page.

## Player Entry

- **Rule 4 Draft (MLB Draft):** Players sign after being drafted and are assigned to affiliates
- **International amateur signings:** Players eligible at age 16; sign during annual windows; frequently begin in DR academy/DSL
- **Independent league acquisitions:** MLB clubs can purchase contracts of independent-league players

(source: [minor-league-baseball-ecosystem.md](../sources/minor-league-baseball-ecosystem.md))

## Season Structure

- Full-season leagues (Low-A through Triple-A) play approximately 132–150 games
- Complex leagues and DSL have shorter, more controlled schedules
- Active roster sizes vary by level (~28 at Triple-A)

## Affiliated Teams

Each MLB organization maintains exactly **4 full-season affiliates** (Triple-A, Double-A, High-A, Single-A), for a total of **120 full-season minor league teams** across all 30 organizations. Three Rookie-level leagues (ACL, FCL, DSL) are shared across organizations rather than individually affiliated. (source: [mlb-milb-teams-by-affiliate.md](../sources/mlb-milb-teams-by-affiliate.md))

For the complete affiliate map organized by MLB parent club and division, see the [MLB Minor League Teams by Affiliate](../sources/mlb-milb-teams-by-affiliate.md) source page.

### Notable Patterns

- Some affiliates share their parent club's name (Iowa Cubs, Syracuse Mets, Dunedin Blue Jays), while most carry unique identities
- Organizations often cluster affiliates geographically for player-movement efficiency
- Post-2020 restructuring standardized the four-tier full-season model across all organizations

## Relationship to MLB

- All MiLB players are under MLB organizational control
- MLB restructured the minor leagues (post-2020), eliminating "short-season" designations
- The first minor league CBA was established, improving player conditions
- The Domestic Reserve List is capped at **165** in-season, creating roster pressure on organizations
