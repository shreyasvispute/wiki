# Major League Baseball (MLB)

Major League Baseball is the premier professional baseball organization in North America, comprising **30 teams** divided into two leagues. (source: [mlb-leagues-divisions-explained.md](../sources/mlb-leagues-divisions-explained.md))

## Structure

- **American League (AL)** — 15 teams across 3 divisions (East, Central, West)
- **National League (NL)** — 15 teams across 3 divisions (East, Central, West)
- The NL is the older of the two leagues, dating to the 19th century
- The AL and NL legally merged into the modern MLB at the end of the 20th century (source: [al-nl-rule-differences.md](../sources/al-nl-rule-differences.md))

### American League Teams

- **AL East:** Baltimore Orioles, Boston Red Sox, New York Yankees, Tampa Bay Rays, Toronto Blue Jays
- **AL Central:** Chicago White Sox, Cleveland Guardians, Detroit Tigers, Kansas City Royals, Minnesota Twins
- **AL West:** Athletics (relocating to Las Vegas by 2028), Houston Astros, Los Angeles Angels, Seattle Mariners, Texas Rangers

### National League Teams

- **NL East:** Atlanta Braves, Miami Marlins, New York Mets, Philadelphia Phillies, Washington Nationals
- **NL Central:** Chicago Cubs, Cincinnati Reds, Milwaukee Brewers, Pittsburgh Pirates, St. Louis Cardinals
- **NL West:** Arizona Diamondbacks, Colorado Rockies, Los Angeles Dodgers, San Diego Padres, San Francisco Giants

## Regular Season

- **162 games** per team
- Teams play most games within their division, with interleague play mixed in
- Mid-summer All-Star Game break

## Postseason

- **12 teams** qualify (6 per league)
- Three division champions per league receive automatic playoff entry
- Three additional wild card teams per league based on best remaining records
- Division champion with the worst record must play in the Wild Card round
- Top two division champions in each league receive a first-round bye

(source: [mlb-leagues-divisions-explained.md](../sources/mlb-leagues-divisions-explained.md))

## Rules

- As of 2026, the AL and NL play under **identical rules**
- The last major rule difference — the [Designated Hitter](../concepts/designated-hitter.md) — was standardized across both leagues in 2022

(source: [al-nl-rule-differences.md](../sources/al-nl-rule-differences.md))

## Minor League System

- MLB controls all [Minor League Baseball](minor-league-baseball.md) affiliates; every minor leaguer is an organization employee
- The minor league hierarchy runs from Rookie/Complex ball through Triple-A
- Key roster mechanisms: 40-man roster, Rule 5 Draft protection, Domestic Reserve List (165 cap), minor league options, service time (172 days = 1 year)
- Public Minor League Statcast data is complete for Triple-A but incomplete at lower levels

(source: [minor-league-baseball-ecosystem.md](../sources/minor-league-baseball-ecosystem.md))
